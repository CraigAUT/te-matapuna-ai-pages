Facilitator scenario cards — one set, print double-sided or single-sided

Block 1 · Research Question
🟢 YES
"You've been given a broad topic. You ask AI: 'What are some different angles I could take on this topic?' — then you choose the one that interests you most."
Values debrief
Tika — the what
Using the right tool for the right task. AI is good at generating options. You're good at knowing what matters to you.
"That's the right division of labour."

Block 1 · Research Question
🟡 IT DEPENDS
"You're stuck on your topic. You ask AI: 'What should my research question be?' — and you use what it gives you."
Values debrief
Pono — the why
Did you interrogate it? Test it? Make it yours? Are you being honest about whose thinking this is?
"A research question that doesn't come from your curiosity is very hard to write 1000 words about."
3
Block 1 · Research Question
🟢 YES
"You draft a research question yourself, then ask AI: 'Is this question too broad, too narrow, or about right for a 1000-word analysis?' You use the feedback to refine it."
Values debrief
Aroha — the how
Caring for your own learning — you did the hard part yourself and used AI to improve it.
"You wrote it, you're evaluating it, you decide what to do with the feedback."
4
Block 2 · Building Your Search
🟢 YES
"You use AI to brainstorm synonyms and related terms for your keywords — then you test those terms yourself in Library Search."
Values debrief
Tika — the what
The right tool at the right moment. The intellectual work of evaluating which results are useful is still yours.
"AI knows a lot of words. You know your topic. Together that's a useful search."
5
Block 2 · Building Your Search
🟡 IT DEPENDS — leaning NO
"You ask Copilot to find you three academic sources on your topic."
Values debrief
Pono — truth & reality
AI can hallucinate references. Does it have access to AUT Library databases? Can it verify those sources exist and are peer-reviewed?
"Citing a source that doesn't exist is fabrication — even if you didn't mean it. Always verify in Library Search."
6
Block 2 · Building Your Search
🔴 NO
"You ask AI to find your three sources, summarise them, and you paste the summaries into your reference list."
Values debrief
Pono
Tika
You haven't read the sources. You don't know if they exist. You don't know if the summary is accurate. This is fabrication risk at every step.
"You're here to learn to read and think. That's the whole point of Stages 2 and 3."
7
Block 2 · Building Your Search
🟢 YES
"Following on from Card 4, you take your keywords and ask AI to combine them into a search string using AND, OR, NOT. You also ask it to explain the string in one sentence so you understand what it's doing. Then you run it yourself in Library Search."
Values debrief
Tika — doing what is needed, in the right way
Asking for the one-sentence explanation is the key move. It's the difference between using a tool and understanding it.
"Next time, you can build the string yourself."
8
Block 3 · Reading & Note-Making
🟡 IT DEPENDS
"You don't understand a concept in your reading. You paste the paragraph into Copilot and ask it to explain it in plain language."
Values debrief
Pono — honest intention
Are you using this to help you read — or instead of reading? Did you go back to the original text afterwards?
"Using AI to unlock a difficult text: that's a reading strategy. Using AI so you never read the text: that's avoiding the learning."
9
Block 3 · Reading & Note-Making
🟢 YES
"After reading a section, you explain the main idea back to Copilot in your own words and ask: 'Have I understood this correctly?'"
Values debrief
Aroha — caring for your own learning
You read, you processed, you tested your understanding. AI is the mirror, not the source.
"This is a more sophisticated use of AI than most students think of. The thinking is yours."
10
Block 3 · Reading & Note-Making
🔴 NO
"You ask Copilot to summarise an article for you. You use the summary as your notes and build your assignment from it."
Values debrief
Whakapapa of ideas
The original author's thinking has been filtered through AI — their voice, evidence, argument is no longer direct. You're citing ideas you haven't engaged with.
"AI summaries can be subtly, confidently wrong. If you haven't read the source, you can't catch that."
11
Block 4 · The Hard Ones
🟡 IT DEPENDS
"You've written a rough first draft. You paste it into Copilot and ask: 'Does my argument make sense? Is there anything missing?'"
Values debrief
Pono
Tika
Getting feedback on your own writing: legitimate. But — did you act on the feedback yourself, or did you ask Copilot to rewrite it?
"Stage 6 is 'receive and apply feedback.' The question is whether the thinking and fixing is yours."
12
Block 4 · The Hard Ones
🔴 NO
"You use AI at various points in your research process. At the end you're not sure exactly what counts as 'AI-generated material' — so you don't declare anything and just submit."
Values debrief
Pono — honesty even when it's unclear
The assessment is clear: any AI-generated material that informed your work must be cited. Uncertainty doesn't remove the obligation — it means ask.
"Declaring too much is never a problem. Declaring nothing when you used something is."

Suggested sequencing

Open with: Cards 1, 4, 10 — clear YES, clear YES, clear NO — to establish the range quickly.

Middle: Cards 8, 5, 11 — genuinely ambiguous, where the values framing does most work.

Close with: Cards 2 and 12 — these generate most debate. Good to end on unresolved territory.

Cards 4 → 7: Run these as a pair to show the workflow connecting. Ask students what changed between the two scenarios.

Before Card 10: Introduce the whakapapa of ideas concept — that every idea has a lineage. The reading scenario lands much harder once students have that frame.

For 30 students: Select 6–8 cards. Recommended: 1, 4, 7, 10, 8, 11, 12.

For 15 students: Run all 12. Physical movement (YES / NO / IT DEPENDS corners) works well at this size.
