Is this OK?

A group sorting activity

What you need

  • 1 set of placement cards for the whole class — YES, NO, IT DEPENDS — displayed at the front of the room or on a central table
  • 2–3 scenario cards per small group, distributed from the shuffled deck so different groups get different scenarios
  • 30 minutes total — see the lesson flow

How to run it

  1. No intro needed — just deal the cards. Don't explain the values first. Give each group 2–3 scenario cards. The 3 placement cards — YES, NO, IT DEPENDS — are displayed at the front for everyone to see. Ask groups to read their scenarios and decide where they'd place them. That's it.
  2. Groups discuss quietly first. One person reads the scenario aloud within the group. They agree on a placement and choose a spokesperson.
  3. Groups come up and place publicly. One group at a time — the spokesperson reads the scenario to the class, places it on the correct card, and gives the group's reason. Then ask the room: "Does everyone agree? Who'd put it somewhere else — and why?" Hold the disagreement — don't resolve it yet.
  4. Introduce the values after the game. Once groups have worked through their cards, introduce pono, tika, aroha as the framework that explains the reasoning they've already been doing.
  5. Card 8 is deliberately contested. Expect real disagreement. That's intentional — hold the tension rather than resolving it quickly.

Facilitation notes

🔵 Pono — the why

Is the work genuinely theirs? Is it truthful and sincere? Does it actually represent who they are and what they know?

🟢 Tika — the what

Did they follow the right process? Have they considered the consequences for everyone involved — including their assessor and classmates?

🩷 Aroha — the how

Is there genuine care here — for their own learning, for their community, for the knowledge they carry? Aroha is relational.
